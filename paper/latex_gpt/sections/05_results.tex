\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Baseline Digital Performance}
\label{subsec:baseline-digital}

Digital FP32 baselines are 95.46\% (ResNet-18), 90.74\% (ConvNeXt-Tiny), and 98.06\% (Tiny-ViT-5M) on CIFAR-10, with corresponding CIFAR-100 values of 78.64\%, 64.12\%, and 86.94\%.

\subsection{Quantization and Noise Resilience}
\label{subsec:quantization-noise}

The zero-noise hybrid control (V2; Table~\ref{tab:exp-notation}) retains 97.39$\pm$0.00\% ($n=10$ deterministic evaluations). The recovered-weight law (Eq.~\ref{eq:scale-recovery}) suppresses much effective analog perturbation below the 4-bit LSB.

On CIFAR-100, HAT improves Tiny-ViT from 44.06\% to 65.48\% and ConvNeXt from 23.86\% to 60.54\%.

The same 6-bit threshold holds on ConvNeXt-Tiny (Supplementary Fig.~\SuppFigNoiseSweep), and the iso-accuracy contour analysis (Section~\ref{subsec:iso-accuracy}) shows this cliff is independent of device variability, producing a consistent $\sim$7~pp transition across all tested $\sigma_{\mathrm{D2D}}$ levels.

\subsection{Retention and Temporal Drift}
\label{subsec:retention-drift}

The corrected retention-aware follow-up model (V8) exhibits a rapid early drop (91.63\% at 0~s $\rightarrow$ 82.66\% at 1~s) followed by a broad plateau near 79\% (79.13--79.51\%) through 10~000~s (Supplementary Fig.~\SuppFigRetention).

\subsection{Hardware-Instance Transferability}
\label{subsec:hardware-transferability}

Standard fixed-mask HAT (V4) collapses to chance level (10.00\%) on arrays with different fixed D2D realizations, indicating pronounced hardware-instance overfitting. The collapse was independently confirmed under no-AMP FP32 inference (Supplementary Note S-Verification). This motivates the distribution-matched Ensemble HAT objective (Eq.~\ref{eq:hat-ensemble-distribution}), formally defined in Section~\ref{sec:methodology} and instantiated in Section~\ref{subsec:nl-hat-stress}.

A canonical class-distribution diagnostic clarifies the collapse mechanism. Re-evaluating the fixed-mask Standard HAT checkpoint on five fresh D2D instances yields $10.00\pm0.00\%$ accuracy; for every instance, all 10\,000 test images are assigned to a single CIFAR-10 class (prediction entropy $\approx0$, maximum-class frequency $=100\%$). Under the same five-instance protocol, canonical Ensemble HAT reaches $85.97\pm1.98\%$ with near-uniform prediction entropy ($2.28$ vs.~$\ln 10$) and a maximum-class frequency of $15.27\pm2.27\%$ (Supplementary Fig.~\SuppFigCollapseMechanism). Thus, the fixed-mask failure is a deterministic single-class hardware-instance collapse rather than Monte Carlo noise or mixed-precision evaluation drift, and epoch-level D2D resampling prevents this degenerate predictor.

\subsection{Physical Front-end Compensation}
\label{subsec:frontend-compensation}

The organic phototransistor front end exhibits a sublinear photoresponse $I_{\text{photo}} \propto P^{\gamma_{\text{phys}}}$ that we compensate with an inverse-gamma preprocessor $P_{\text{in}} = X^{1/\gamma_{\text{phys}}}$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:inverse-gamma}). Compensation restores \textbf{+5.8~pp} at $\gamma_{\text{phys}}=2.0$ (89.85\% vs.\ 84.04\%, single-seed deterministic evaluation), but amplifies shot-noise variance for $\gamma_{\text{phys}} < 1$. The full $\gamma_{\text{phys}}$ scan and the analytical trade-off are shown in Supplementary Figs.~\SuppFigFrontend--\SuppFigSnr.

\subsection{Iso-Accuracy Operating Envelope}
\label{subsec:iso-accuracy}

A 63-point joint sweep over $\sigma_{\mathrm{D2D}} \in \{1,3,5,8,10,15,20\}\%$ and ADC $\in \{2,3,4,5,6,7,8,10,12\}$ bits (10 MC runs per point) reveals three regimes (Figure~\ref{fig:contour-map}). Below 5-bit ADC, accuracy collapses to near-chance; the 5-to-6-bit transition yields a consistent $\sim$7~pp jump. Above 6 bits, degradation is dominated by D2D: $\leq$10\% D2D maintains $>$84\%, while 20\% D2D reduces it to 71--77\%. Sobol indices (Eq.~\ref{eq:sobol-first-order}) are $S_{\mathrm{ADC}}=0.98$ over the full grid and $S_{\mathrm{D2D}}=0.92$ in the operational region ($\geq$6~bits, $\sigma_{\mathrm{D2D}} \leq 15\%$), with negligible interaction ($<$4\%).

\subsection{Nonlinear Writing and Hardware-Aware Training}
\label{subsec:nl-hat-stress}

Proportional-noise HAT recovers Tiny-ViT to 97.37$\pm$0.05\% when training and evaluation physics are aligned, but falls to 10.38$\pm$0.44\% under uniform-noise semantics. For ConvNeXt-Tiny, proportional-noise HAT reaches 91.98\% (91.91$\pm$0.08\% Monte Carlo).

Ensemble HAT (Figure~\ref{fig:ensemble-hat-concept}; Eq.~\ref{eq:hat-ensemble-distribution}) preserves 86.16$\pm$0.19\% across three protocol-matched training seeds under canonical uniform noise ($NL=1.0$), with each seed evaluated on 10 fresh arrays and five Monte Carlo passes per array. The original plotted canonical checkpoint remains 86.37$\pm$1.54\% across its 10 fresh arrays, and all three seeds remain far above standard fixed-mask HAT ($10.00\pm0.00\%$). An ablation scan (Supplementary Fig.~\SuppFigZeroShot) shows epoch-level resampling reaches 88.41\% (50-epoch), versus 87.18\% (fixed) and 86.16\% (per-batch), confirming structured epoch-level resampling as the load-bearing schedule choice.

An intermediate NL sweep (Ensemble HAT, seed 123, uniform noise) bridges the canonical and severe regimes. At $NL=1.2$ the fresh-instance mean is $83.03\pm0.22\%$; at $NL=1.5$ it is $82.63\pm0.10\%$; and at $NL=1.8$ it falls to $80.31\pm0.40\%$ (Supplementary Table~\ref{tab:r10d-nl-interpolation}). The degradation is gradual rather than abrupt, with the largest drop occurring between $NL=1.5$ and $NL=1.8$. This monotonic trend supports the interpretation that the $\sim$5~pp residual gap at $NL=2.0$ reflects a training-recipe limitation in the strongly nonlinear regime, not a physical accuracy floor.

Four observations emerge from the M-series evidence (seeds 123, 456, 789). First, all true-NL2 routes recover to $\sim$80--82\%, irrespective of noise law or D2D resampling. Second, Standard and Ensemble HAT do not separate within seed variance (Standard uniform $81.20\%$ vs Ensemble uniform $80.54\%$). Third, proportional noise offers no advantage under $NL=2.0$ ($80.83\%$ vs uniform $80.54\%$). Fourth, 8-bit ADC quantization does not materially alter the headline ($-0.10$~pp), while a 6-bit spot-check reveals a $-2.8$~pp cliff, consistent with \S\ref{subsec:iso-accuracy}.

Severe-NL retraining recovers to $\sim$80--82\%, leaving a residual gap of roughly 15~pp to the digital baseline.

\subsection{Case Study: Zero-Shot Transfer to a Literature-Calibrated Device}
\label{subsec:case-study}

We simulated zero-shot transfer to a 2025 OPECT array \citep{zhang2025opect} ($\sigma_{\mathrm{C2C}}=2\%, \sigma_{\mathrm{D2D}}=3\%$). Figure~\ref{fig:case-study-transfer} shows that standard-HAT checkpoints transfer poorly: ConvNeXt retains partial transfer, whereas Tiny-ViT V4 collapses to chance level (10.00\%) across all alternatives. By contrast, the Ensemble-HAT checkpoint reaches \textbf{88.53$\pm$0.08\%} ($n=10$ fresh-instance evaluations) under the nominal OPECT proxy.

    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.88\textwidth]{fig10_zero_shot_transferability}
    \caption{\textbf{Zero-shot transfer across alternative device profiles.} Standard-HAT checkpoints are evaluated on literature-calibrated or measured alternative profiles without profile-specific retraining. ConvNeXt C4 retains partial transfer on OPECT and idealized profiles but degrades on PCM and RRAM; Tiny-ViT V4 collapses to chance level (10.00\%) across all alternatives. By contrast, Ensemble-HAT reaches $88.53\pm0.08\%$ on the OPECT proxy. Dashed vertical lines mark source-profile best accuracy; n/a indicates unavailable combinations.}
    \label{fig:case-study-transfer}
\end{figure}

The OPECT case study \citep{zhang2025opect} provides \textbf{one literature-anchored validation point}; we choose it because it is the most complete published organic-array characterization to date, with documented D2D, C2C, and conductance-window statistics. The framework is \textbf{profile-agnostic by design}: any measured device statistics can be ingested through the documented profile interface (Supplementary Note S-HW). We anticipate cross-validation against additional fabricated profiles in a companion study; the present zero-shot transfer demonstrates the framework's portability without retraining, not a claim that OPECT is universally representative.
