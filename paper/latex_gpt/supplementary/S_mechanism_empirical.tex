\section*{Supplementary Note S-Mechanism: Empirical Landscape Analyses}
\label{supp:mechanism-empirical}

This note presents empirical analyses that complement the theoretical arguments in Supplementary Note~S-Theory. The analyses were performed on existing evaluated checkpoints without retraining, using the protocol documented in \texttt{scripts/\_gpt/empirical\_mechanism\_20260425.py}. Provenance (commit hash, checkpoint paths, GPU device) is archived in JSON outputs under \texttt{report\_md/\_gpt/json\_gpt/}.

\subsection*{S-M.1 D2D-direction loss-landscape interpolation (E2)}

We interpolate between the checkpoint's training D2D mask $\mathbf{M}_{\text{train}}$ and a fresh mask $\mathbf{M}_{\text{fresh}}$ via $\mathbf{M}(\alpha) = \mathbf{M}_{\text{train}} + \alpha(\mathbf{M}_{\text{fresh}} - \mathbf{M}_{\text{train}})$, with $\alpha \in \{0, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 2.5, 3\}$. The table reports headline points only; the figure shows the full sweep. The $\alpha=0$ point recovers the training mask; $\alpha=1$ is a fresh instance; $\alpha=3$ extrapolates beyond the training distribution.

\begin{table}[ht]
\centering
\caption{D2D loss-landscape interpolation for canonical Standard vs Ensemble HAT ($NL=1.0$).}
\label{tab:e2-landscape}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{$\alpha=0$ (train mask)} & \textbf{$\alpha=1$ (fresh)} & \textbf{$\alpha=3$ (extrapolated)} \\
\midrule
Standard HAT & 94.67\% & 10.00\% & 9.55\% \\
Ensemble HAT & 91.25\% & 88.39\% & 27.06\% \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\begin{figure}[ht]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figS_d2d_loss_landscape}
    \caption{\textbf{D2D-direction loss-landscape interpolation (E2).} Test accuracy (left axis, solid lines with markers) and test loss (right axis, dashed lines with crosses) for canonical Standard HAT and Ensemble HAT as a function of interpolation parameter $\alpha$ from the training mask ($\alpha=0$) toward a fresh mask ($\alpha=1$) and beyond ($\alpha=3$). Standard HAT collapses to chance level at $\alpha=1$, whereas Ensemble HAT maintains $88.39\%$. Shaded regions denote $\pm 1$ standard deviation across three sampled fresh masks.}
    \label{fig:e2-landscape}
\end{figure}

Table~\ref{tab:e2-landscape} and Figure~\ref{fig:e2-landscape} show that Ensemble HAT maintains $88.39\%$ on fresh masks, whereas Standard HAT collapses to chance level ($10.00\%$). Even under $3\times$ extrapolated mismatch, Ensemble HAT outperforms Standard HAT by $17.5$~pp. This is the primary empirical evidence that per-epoch resampling yields D2D-directional robustness.

Four of the five most sensitive layers are MLP layers, supporting the main-text claim that severe-NL failure localizes primarily to the MLP path. The patch-embedding convolution (rank~2) is also critical, consistent with its role as the first analog operation in the inference path.

\subsection*{S-M.3 Full-parameter-space Hessian spectra (E1)}

We approximate the Hessian eigenspectrum of the training loss over analog parameters using Lanczos iteration with Hessian-vector products (HVPs). The top absolute Ritz eigenvalue provides a proxy for the sharpest curvature direction.

\begin{table}[ht]
\centering
\caption{Top-1 absolute Hessian eigenvalue by checkpoint.}
\label{tab:e1-hessian}
\begin{tabular}{lc}
\toprule
\textbf{Checkpoint} & \textbf{Top-1 abs Ritz eigenvalue} \\
\midrule
Canonical Ensemble HAT ($NL=1.0$) & 221.30 \\
Canonical Standard HAT ($NL=1.0$) & 23.28 \\
M1 Standard ($NL=2.0$) & 30{,}058.40 \\
M2 Ensemble ($NL=2.0$) & 5{,}705.60 \\
M3 Proportional ($NL=2.0$) & 1{,}764.97 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\begin{figure}[ht]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figS_hessian_spectrum}
    \caption{\textbf{Hessian eigenspectrum approximation (E1).} Absolute Ritz eigenvalues (log scale) from Lanczos iteration over analog parameters for five checkpoints. At $NL=1.0$, Standard HAT exhibits a lower top eigenvalue ($23.28$) than Ensemble HAT ($221.30$), but this mask-specific ``flatness'' does not predict fresh-instance transferability (Table~\ref{tab:e2-landscape}). The $NL=2.0$ severe-NL checkpoints (M1--M3) show dramatically larger eigenvalues, consistent with loss-surface instability under strong write nonlinearity.}
    \label{fig:e1-hessian}
\end{figure}

Standard HAT exhibits a lower top eigenvalue ($23.28$) than Ensemble HAT ($221.30$) at the canonical $NL=1.0$ regime. However, this ``flatness'' is mask-specific: Standard HAT collapses on fresh masks (Table~\ref{tab:e2-landscape}), indicating that full-parameter-space curvature does not predict fresh-instance transferability. The relevant metric is D2D-directional sensitivity rather than global curvature. The $NL=2.0$ severe-NL checkpoints show dramatically larger absolute top eigenvalues ($1.76\times10^{3}$--$3.01\times10^{4}$), consistent with increased loss-surface instability under strong write nonlinearity.

\textit{Limitation.} The Lanczos approximation uses a fixed eval batch of 32 samples. For $4.73\times10^{6}$ analog parameters, this may underestimate tail eigenvalues. The ranking across checkpoints is nevertheless informative.

\subsection*{S-M.4 Cross-checkpoint representational similarity (E3)}

Linear Centered Kernel Alignment (CKA) across the M-series checkpoints (42 common analog layers) yields an aggregate off-diagonal CKA of $0.455$. This mixed value indicates that different seeds and noise laws do not converge to identical representations; the $\sim$80--82\% severe-NL recovery band is achieved through multiple distinct representational routes.

\subsection*{S-M.5 Checkpoint averaging ablation (E5)}

Naive parameter-space averaging of two Standard HAT checkpoints (M1 seed~123 and M5 seed~456) yields $10.00\pm0.00\%$ on fresh instances---identical to chance level. This confirms that Ensemble HAT's robustness does not stem from simple parameter smoothing, but from the training objective's explicit mismatch-distribution averaging.

\begin{figure}[ht]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.65\textwidth]{figS_checkpoint_avg}
    \caption{\textbf{Checkpoint averaging ablation (E5).} Fresh-instance accuracy for Standard HAT seed~123, Standard HAT seed~456, their naive parameter-space average, and the Ensemble HAT reference. Averaging two Standard checkpoints yields chance-level performance ($10.00\%$), confirming that per-epoch D2D resampling is not equivalent to simple parameter-space smoothing.}
    \label{fig:e5-avg}
\end{figure}
