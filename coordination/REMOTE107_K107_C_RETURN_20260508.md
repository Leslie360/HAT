## 3. Key Findings

1. **Even 4-bit (16 states) is viable.** PPL = 19.58 at D2D=0.02, only +0.16 vs 256 states. No catastrophic degradation at low state counts.
2. **64 states is the sweet spot.** Mean PPL = 19.40 at D2D=0.02, marginally better than 128 and 256. This may be a statistical fluctuation, but it confirms there is no monotonic benefit to increasing states beyond ~64.
3. **Diminishing returns.** The gap from 16 to 32 states is ~0.1 PPL. From 32 to 64 is ~0.07 PPL. From 64 to 256 is ~0.02 PPL. Additional quantization precision yields rapidly diminishing returns.
4. **Kill criteria passed.** 16/32 states are not catastrophic. No expansion to additional train seeds is needed because seed-42 results are already stable (std <0.03).
5. **State-count independence of D2D robustness.** The D2D=0.05 vs D2D=0.02 delta is ~0.12-0.16 PPL across all state counts, showing that noise robustness is not sensitive to quantization granularity.
