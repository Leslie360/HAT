# Gemini Desk-Reject Defense — 2026-04-19

## 1. "Scope is too narrow for Nature Communications"
Response: The manuscript is explicitly positioned as a materials-to-system decision aid for edge vision rather than an ImageNet-scale deployment claim. The cross-disciplinary value lies in connecting organic-device assumptions to modern ViT/CNN task outcomes, which is stated in the abstract and introduction.

## 2. "No fabricated-array validation"
Response: The paper repeatedly discloses that it is first-order behavioral and not chip-predictive, and now includes sensitivity analysis, no-AMP integrity checks, and correlated-D2D robustness to bound the main claims. The lack of fabricated-array closure is framed as a scope boundary, not hidden as a missing validation.

## 3. "Mitigation claims are too broad"
Response: The severe-NL lane has been explicitly demoted to a supplementary diagnostic result because fresh-instance transfer remains weak even for all-linear controls. This prevents overclaiming and shows the authors are separating diagnosis from deployment-grade mitigation.
