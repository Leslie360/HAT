# KIMI Editor Abstract Variant — 2026-04-19

Organic optoelectronic CIM devices are often evaluated at the device level, but their deployment limits on modern vision models remain unclear. This work shows that the dominant failure mode is not nominal conductance quantization but hardware-instance overfitting: standard hardware-aware training collapses to 10.00% on fresh arrays, whereas Ensemble HAT restores 86.37±1.54%. The framework also identifies a 6-bit ADC threshold, bounds severe nonlinear-write recovery to a diagnostic supplementary result, and shows that moderate spatially correlated D2D mismatch causes bounded degradation rather than catastrophic failure. Together these results establish a behavioral materials-to-system decision aid for edge-vision deployment before fabricated array closure.
