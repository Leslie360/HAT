\paragraph{Remote107 adaptive-schedule validation lane.}
The newer 107-clean push extends the 410M selective-KV result into a validation lane on training schedules and larger models, but it should remain below the main claim-bearing table until it has a source-data package comparable to the 2026-05-11 claim-lock packet. Within the frozen-target 410M schedule study, fixed, cosine, and layer-wise training reach post-HAT PPL values of 18.75, 18.31, and 18.36, while reverse layer-wise degrades to 21.30, indicating that concentrating stronger regularization on late layers is more effective than emphasizing early layers. At larger scales, the three main schedules nearly tie: Pythia-2.8B gives 12.68/12.68/12.70 and Pythia-6.9B gives 11.40/11.38/11.43 for fixed/cosine/layer-wise. The clearest additional gain appears when the analog scope expands beyond a single terminal layer: on Pythia-2.8B, \texttt{last2+cosine} reaches 12.56 PPL, improving over \texttt{last2+fixed} at 13.78 and slightly outperforming \texttt{last1+fixed} at 12.68. These rows strengthen the engineering conclusion that schedule design matters most when analog exposure grows, while leaving the main manuscript claim unchanged: terminal-layer selectivity is the viable route, and all-layer analog KV remains the negative control.
